Helm

God of Death, Keeper of Peace

Helm has been the eternal sentry and was always seen wearing a full suit of armor that represented the weight of his heavy responsibility - the ending of life. As mortals grow old, and gain power, they corrupt, the mortal soul was never meant to live an undying life. There are four planes in Helm's Realm: The Nine Hells, Elysium, The Fugue Plane, and Limbo

The Nine Hells

A Place of War and Punishment

“From the boiling rivers of fluid in Avernus to the sheer crags of Nessus, you'd be hard-pressed to find a place that sums up the cold cruelty of the planes more than The Hells do.”
  - Nomoto Sinh, a subject decaying in Minauros

The Nine Hells (sometimes referred to as Baator) are a plane of sinister wickedness and institutional cruelty, its denizens organized into a strict caste system with a very rigid chain of command. Each of the nine Hells had its own physical laws or properties of matter, but all were inhospitable or deadly to outsiders.

Elysium

Dying a Hero's Death

“The path you travel doesn't matter. You can walk in a straight, orderly line, or race chaotically this way and that. Just keep the place you want to get to in mind and start walking. As long as you do good works along the way, you'll reach your destination.”
  - Rion, a shepherd of Elysium.

Elysium is a plane embodying the concept of pure good without being overly restricted by law nor dissipated by the randomness of chaos. Any who have lived a righteous and proper life can end it here, but the great heroes have a reserved seat.

The Fugue Plane

Caught Between Good and Evil

"Should you get a chance to live your life over again, perhaps try something more interesting"
  - Kelemvor, a ruler of The Fugue Plane.

The Fugue Plane is home to the most in the middle of all souls. No allegiences pledged, no wars fought, no crimes committed. Here they are offered a chance to plead their case and find their permanent afterlife. The vast majority of this plane is flat, gray, bland and nondescript, with no notable topographical features. The sky overhead is also gray. The plane's only significant feature is The City of Judgment, in the middle of which stood The Crystal Spire where Kelemvor and Jergal reside and rule.

Limbo

An Inconceivable Conundrum

"Breathe the fire; Walk the air; Drink the earth; Warm your hands at the water."
  - A petitioner's greeting to Limbo.

This plane is supreme chaos, a twisting, quicksilver place filled with bits and pieces of rocks, trees, the four elements, entire landscapes, strong winds, and random pockets of liquid, solid, or gas. Sentient beings can dampen down the chaos to livable levels and produce a safe environment as long as they maintain concentration. The size of a safe zone is directly related to the intelligence of the individual controller.
